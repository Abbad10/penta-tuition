Course Curriculum

Beginner Level

Introduction to basic Chess fundamentals. Students are ready to play with any adult after completing this module.

  • Introduction to the Chessboard
  • Chessboard ranks and files
  • Importance of setting up the board correctly
  • Basic moves for chess pieces
  • The concept of check, illegal moves, and pawn promotion
  • Castling: definition, purpose, and conditions
  • The concept of protection and good/bad captures
  • Practice: checkmate in one move
  • Checkmating a lone King with King and Queen
  • 1-on-1 Classes: 25 hours live sessions
  • Tournaments, puzzles, quizzes, and regular feedback

Intermediate Level

Roadmap to ace school and state-level tournaments with real-life game tactics and strategic preparations.

  • The concept of counting: good and bad trades
  • Basic chess strategy: defense and safe moves
  • Opening and middlegame tactics: saving key pieces
  • Basic tactics: pawn fork, Knight fork, double attack
  • Basic tactics: skewers, pins, discovered check
  • Checkmating a lone King with a King and a Rook
  • Basic endgame strategies
  • 1-on-1 Classes: 30 hours live sessions
  • Tournaments, puzzles, quizzes, and regular feedback

Advanced Level

Path to becoming a FIDE-rated chess player and ace national-level chess tournaments.

  • Complex examples of forks, pins, skewers, and discovered check
  • Advanced tactical tools: clearance, interference, double check
  • Essential opening theory and frequent checkmating patterns
  • Advanced middlegame strategy: weak squares and Knight outposts
  • Essential endgame knowledge: Bishop and Rook endgames
  • 1-on-1 Classes: 36 hours live sessions
  • Tournaments, puzzles, quizzes, and regular feedback

What skills you will gain from Chess

Critical Thinking Skills

Chess encourages kids to think strategically, plan ahead, and anticipate their opponent’s moves. This strengthens their ability to analyze situations, weigh options, and make thoughtful decisions.

Memory Enhancement

Playing chess sharpens memory by requiring kids to remember strategies, recognize patterns, and recall past moves. This mental exercise helps boost their cognitive abilities and retention skills.

Concentration & Focus

Chess requires intense focus as players analyze moves and plan their next steps. Kids develop better attention spans and mental discipline, which positively impact their academic and everyday life.

Pattern Recognition

Through chess, children learn to spot patterns, visualize outcomes, and identify potential threats. These skills enhance their analytical thinking and problem-solving abilities.

Strategic Planning

Chess teaches kids to think long-term by analyzing positions and planning strategies. This ability to anticipate and adapt is a valuable life skill, preparing them for challenges beyond the board.

Time Management

Competitive chess enhances time management by training kids to make optimal moves under time pressure, improving efficiency and quick thinking in exams and daily tasks.

Social & Emotional Skills

Chess promotes social skills by encouraging communication, teamwork, and discussions about strategies. It also instills sportsmanship, teaching kids to celebrate wins humbly and handle losses gracefully.

Problem-Solving Abilities

By analyzing multiple possibilities, adapting strategies, and anticipating opponent’s moves, chess strengthens children's ability to think critically and solve real-world problems.
