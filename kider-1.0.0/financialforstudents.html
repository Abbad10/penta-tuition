Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our financial literacy program, iQued Finfit.

iQued Finfit is a financial literacy program designed to help students and young individuals understand and manage money effectively. It covers essential topics like budgeting, saving, investing, and managing debt. The program aims to equip students with practical financial skills that they can use throughout their lives.

Financial literacy is an essential life skill that is often overlooked in traditional education. iQued Finfit helps students gain a deep understanding of money management, which is crucial for making informed decisions about budgeting, saving, and investing. By learning these skills early, your child will be better equipped to handle their finances in the future and make smart financial choices.

The iQued Finfit course is designed for students aged 12 and above, including teenagers and young adults. It is suitable for those who are just beginning to learn about money management, as well as those who wish to deepen their understanding of personal finance. The course content is tailored to suit various learning stages.

The iQued Finfit course is delivered through interactive online sessions with expert instructors. These sessions are personalized and conducted 1-on-1 to ensure each student receives individualized attention. Our course materials are accessible online and include engaging lessons, quizzes, and practical exercises.

The iQued Finfit course covers a variety of important financial topics, including:
  • Budgeting and managing personal expenses
  • Saving and setting up emergency funds
  • Investing basics (stocks, bonds, mutual funds)
  • Understanding credit, loans, and debt management
  • Planning for future financial goals (education, career, etc.)
Our goal is to equip students with a comprehensive understanding of money management that they can apply in real life.
